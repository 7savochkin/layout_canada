Itinerary

Day 1

Toronto

On arrival, pick up your car hire and make way to your downtown hotel. Rest of the day at leisure.

Day 2

Toronto

Explore the many identities of this cosmopolitan city.

Day 3

Toronto to Kingston

Head to Kingston and tour Fort Henry. An alternative route to Kingston passes through the quaint heritage towns of Prince Edward County.

Day 4

Kingston to Montréal

En route to Montréal today, you will be entering the beautiful 1000 Islands region, where forested islands lie amid tranquil river-ways.

Day 5

Montréal

Montréal is renowned for its active and cosmopolitan nightlife, Visit Notre-Dame Basilica, Old Montréal, Mt. Royal Park, or the underground city

Day 6

Montréal to Québec City

En route to Québec City, we suggest a stop at Cap-de-la-Madeleine to visit its famous basilica.

Day 7

Québec City

On arrival, pick up your car hire and make way to your downtown hotel. Rest of the day at leisure.

Day 8

Québec City to Ottawa

Visit more local attractions, perhaps the Wolfe-Montcalm Monument or Dufferin Terrace. Drive one of several routes to Ottawa, the nation’s capital.

Day 9

Ottawa

Use the entire day to see this historic city of pageantry and the arts. We recommend the UNESCO World Heritage Site of the Rideau Canal.

Day 10

Ottawa to Huntsville

Head to Huntsville, close to the forested Algonquin Park. Spend the rest of your day taking in the natural beauty of the area

Day 11

Huntsville

Why not take a leisurely canoe trip into Algonquin Park’s awe-inspiring back country? Or take an invigorating hike in nearby Arrowhead Park.

Day 12

Huntsville to Niagara Falls

Drive south and bypass Toronto en route to Niagara Falls. Why not pre-book a cruise or helicopter flight to enjoy a close-up of the falls?

Day 13

Niagara Falls to Toronto - End of Tour

Drive to Toronto through Ontario’s fruit-growing region. En route, visit historic Niagara-on-the-Lake and stop at the Welland Canal. Return to
Toronto Pearson International Airport.

Requirements to enter Canada

If you decide to visit Canada by air, you must obtain an Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA). This is a prerequisite if you do not have a Canadian visa or a permanent resident card. If you have Canadian citizenship, you do not need to apply for an eTA. However, you need to present a Canadian passport.If you plan to arrive in Canada by land or sea, you do not need to apply for an eTA.These requirements apply to both tourists and transit passengers.
